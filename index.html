My Story

What shaped me the most were the places where work felt real - construction sites, student teams, and moments where clarity and responsibility mattered more than titles. That’s where I first understood how quality is built, not just inspected.

Through roles like Outreach & PR Lead at TEDxCUSAT, curating an innovation summit, and working with IGBC on sustainability initiatives, I learned how to manage people, communicate openly, and stay steady when plans shift. Those experiences taught me that progress often comes from simple things: clear communication, organised workflows, and a team that trusts each other.

Teaching physics added another layer. It taught me to break concepts down, stay patient, and notice details others might overlook; habits that naturally carry into quality-focused engineering.

Right now, I’m strengthening my foundations in QA and QC, learning how small checks, clean documentation, and practical thinking can make projects run smoother and safer.
